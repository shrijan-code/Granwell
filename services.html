What we do

Support at Home, explained in plain terms.

The Support at Home program groups services into categories. Here's what each one covers, and what Granwell actually delivers for you day to day.

Category 1

At home, day to day

The practical things that keep a home running and a person fed, mobile and safe — without you having to arrange it all yourself.

  • Domestic assistance — cleaning, laundry, general tidying
  • Home maintenance & minor repairs
  • Meal preparation and delivery
  • Transport to appointments, shopping and social outings
Category 2

Making the home work for you

Assistive Technology and Home Modifications (AT-HM) is funded through a separate scheme from your regular Support at Home budget, so using it doesn't reduce what's available for your everyday services. It's assessed as part of your standard aged care assessment — no separate application needed. Funding is split into three tiers based on need:

  • Low-cost items (up to $513/year) — e.g. non-slip bath mats and simple aids, no prescription needed
  • Medium-cost items (up to $2,052/year) — e.g. commode chairs and recliners, usually benefit from professional input
  • High-cost or complex modifications (up to $15,390/year) — e.g. bathroom modifications and major mobility equipment, requires an OT or physio prescription

These figures are current as at 1 July 2026 but are indexed and reviewed regularly by the Australian Government — check with My Aged Care or your care partner for the amounts that apply to you.

Category 3

Staying connected

Support for the things that keep a home liveable and a person part of their community — not just physically cared for, but genuinely connected.

  • Social support & community engagement
  • Accompanied outings and group activities

Need hoarding or squalor support? This isn't part of Support at Home — it's delivered through a separate program (CHSP). If this is something you or a family member needs, get in touch and we'll help you find the right service and understand how it fits alongside anything Granwell provides.

Category 4

Care that's organised for you

Your care partner keeps the whole picture in view — your budget, your providers, your changing needs — so you're not the one holding it all together.

  • Dedicated care management
  • Coordinating services across multiple providers
  • Reviewing and adjusting your care plan as things change
  • Being the one point of contact when something needs sorting
Common questions

Support at Home, answered

Am I eligible for Support at Home?

Eligibility is assessed through My Aged Care. If you're not already assessed, we can point you to the right first step — get in touch and we'll walk you through it.

What does Support at Home cost?

Costs depend on your assessed funding level and any income-tested contribution set by Services Australia. We'll go through what applies to your situation before you commit to anything.

Can I switch to Granwell from my current provider?

Yes. Changing Support at Home providers is a normal, supported process. We'll help you understand the steps and timing so the switch is smooth.

What's the difference between the Support at Home categories?

Category 1 covers everyday practical help like domestic assistance, meals and transport. Category 2 covers assistive technology and home modifications (AT-HM), funded separately from your regular budget. Category 3 covers staying connected, including social support and community engagement. Category 4 is care management — coordinating your services and reviewing your plan as your needs change.

Do I need a care manager if I only want help around the house?

No — you can access Category 1 services on their own. Many people add care management (Category 4) later, once they're coordinating more than one type of support.

Are the service categories the same as my funding level?

No — these are different things. The categories above describe the types of services Granwell provides. Separately, once you're assessed through My Aged Care, you're given your own funding classification from 1 to 8, based on your care needs — this determines your budget, not which provider you choose. Classification 1 is for people who need occasional help with light tasks; Classification 8 is for people with complex, high-level care needs. Your funding is paid quarterly, and 10% of it goes toward care management — coordinating and reviewing your services — with the remaining 90% available for the services themselves. Your care partner will help you understand your classification and how to make the most of your funding across any of Granwell's service categories, including AT-HM (Category 2), which has its own separate funding on top of your regular budget.

What about hoarding or squalor support?

Hoarding and squalor support isn't part of Support at Home — it's delivered through a separate program (CHSP). If this is something you or a family member needs, get in touch and we'll help you find the right service and understand how it fits alongside anything Granwell provides.
