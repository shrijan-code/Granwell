What we do

Support at Home, explained in plain terms.

The Support at Home program groups services into categories. Here's what each one covers, and what Granwell actually delivers for you day to day.

Category 1

At home, day to day

The practical things that keep a home running and a person fed, mobile and safe — without you having to arrange it all yourself.

  • Domestic assistance — cleaning, laundry, general tidying
  • Home maintenance & minor repairs
  • Meal preparation and delivery
  • Transport to appointments, shopping and social outings
Category 3

Staying connected

Support for the things that keep a home liveable and a person part of their community — not just physically cared for, but genuinely connected.

  • Social support & community engagement
  • Accompanied outings and group activities
  • Hoarding & squalor assistance
Category 4

Care that's organised for you

Your care partner keeps the whole picture in view — your budget, your providers, your changing needs — so you're not the one holding it all together.

  • Dedicated care management
  • Coordinating services across multiple providers
  • Reviewing and adjusting your care plan as things change
  • Being the one point of contact when something needs sorting
Common questions

Support at Home, answered

Am I eligible for Support at Home?

Eligibility is assessed through My Aged Care. If you're not already assessed, we can point you to the right first step — get in touch and we'll walk you through it.

What does Support at Home cost?

Costs depend on your assessed funding level and any income-tested contribution set by Services Australia. We'll go through what applies to your situation before you commit to anything.

Can I switch to Granwell from my current provider?

Yes. Changing Support at Home providers is a normal, supported process. We'll help you understand the steps and timing so the switch is smooth.

What's the difference between the Support at Home categories?

Category 1 covers everyday practical help like domestic assistance, meals and transport. Category 3 covers staying connected, including social support and hoarding or squalor assistance. Category 4 is care management — coordinating your services and reviewing your plan as your needs change.

Do I need a care manager if I only want help around the house?

No — you can access Category 1 services on their own. Many people add care management (Category 4) later, once they're coordinating more than one type of support.

Not sure which category fits?

Tell us a bit about your situation and we'll help you work out what support makes sense.
