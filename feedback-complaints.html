Feedback & Complaints

Good or bad, feedback helps us do this better. Here's how to reach us — and how to raise a concern with the regulator directly, at any time.

Your rights

Your rights are set out in the Aged Care Act 2024 Statement of Rights, the Australian Government's official summary of what you're entitled to expect from any aged care provider. We'll give you a copy when you start with us, and you can ask for one at any time.

Talk to us first, if you're comfortable

Most concerns are easiest to sort out directly. You can:

  • Email complaints@granwell.com.au at any time
  • Call us on [ADD: phone number]
  • Speak with your care partner directly, if you already have one

We'll acknowledge your feedback promptly and let you know what happens next. You're welcome to raise a concern anonymously if you'd prefer.

What happens after you raise something

  • We listen and confirm we understand the issue
  • We look into what happened and, where something has gone wrong, explain what we'll do differently
  • We follow up with you once it's resolved

You can also go straight to the regulator

You don't need to raise something with us first. You can contact the Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission (ACQSC) directly, at any time, about any provider:

  • Phone: 1800 951 822
  • Online: agedcarequality.gov.au/contact-us/complaints-concerns
  • An interpreter can be arranged through the Translating and Interpreting Service on 131 450 — ask for the Commission

Raising something with the Commission won't affect the care or services you receive.

Advocacy support

If you'd like independent support to raise a concern, the Older Persons Advocacy Network (OPAN) provides free, confidential advocacy for older Australians accessing aged care services. Call the Aged Care Advocacy Line on 1800 700 600 (8am–8pm weekdays, 10am–4pm Saturdays) to be connected with your state or territory's advocacy service.

Prefer to just talk it through?

Reach out and we'll listen — no forms required.
